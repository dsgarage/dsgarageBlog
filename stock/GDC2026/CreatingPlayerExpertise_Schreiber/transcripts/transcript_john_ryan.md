### What is Narrative Coherence?（#48）
**"It is the art and craft of keeping the player informed and invested in the game's narrative*."**

\* While smartly designed with game design and mechanics.

→ ナラティブ・コヒーレンス = ゲームデザインとメカニクスと巧みに連携させながら、プレイヤーをゲームのナラティブに引き込み続ける技術

### Every Game Has a Narrative（#49）
**"Yes, even Space Invaders"**
- エイリアン: "At last, I will have my revenge!"
- プレイヤー（砲台）: "If I defeat my demons, maybe I can patch things up with my son."
- → **すべてのゲームにナラティブがある**。明示的でなくても。

### Lesson #1: Fable 2（#50）
| | 内容 |
|:---|:---|
| **Problem** | You'll get killed/miss something fun if you don't pay attention in the field |
| **Solution #1** | Designers built **sparkle path** that cue player about the world around them while writers/audio used **ambient narrative** to draw in the player |
| **Solution #2** | **Non-crucial flavor text** saved for menu or pre-release official website |
| **Result** | Story and world feel more organic/more opportunity for player immersion |

### Lesson #2: Guild Wars 2（#51）
| | 内容 |
|:---|:---|
| **Problem** | (See left image) — 戦闘中の情報過多 |
| **Solution #1** | **Bare-bones narrative** for moment-to-moment gameplay: focus on quests steps, player/enemy UI, combat |
| **Solution #2** | **Designed safe zones** where players can get bearings — in-depth lore journal to keep up with years of narrative |
| **Result** | Players can focus on the action and later read their voluminous, studio-accurate lore |

### Lesson #3: Destiny（#52）
| | 内容 |
|:---|:---|
| **Problem** | Same as GW2 |
| **Solution #1** | **Design and writing** recognizing strategies that worked in one genre could work for a different genre |
| **Solution #2** | **Remembering** the action arena/safe zone dynamic in expansions |
| **Result** | We made our lives - and the players' - easier by not reinventing the wheel |

### Lesson #4: Forza Motorsport 7（#53）
| | 内容 |
|:---|:---|
| **Problem** | **Telling a story** while staying out of the player's way/staying within expectations |
| **Solution** | **A light touch**, merging expectation and experience. Front-loaded narrative — the rest is the player, from track to garage |
| **Result** | A player-centric experience that was augmented with automotive realism, the thrill of speed, and minimal UI in the car |

### Lesson #5: Horizon Forbidden [West]（#54）
| | 内容 |
|:---|:---|
| **Problem** | **Informing the player** in a hectic and lush open-world environment |
| **Solution #1** | **Brief UI** gives map marker for location, items, gameplay tips |
| **Solution #2** | **NPCs and audio logs** placed away from combat areas to not take awareness away |
| **Result** | Story and gameplay are a real-time revelation for Aloy and player |

### Lesson #6: Lily's Garden（#55）
| | 内容 |
|:---|:---|
| **Problem** | **Telling an episodic story** on mobile with no lore resource for the player |
| **Solution #1** | **Narrative Design** reminding player of plot and characters at the start or end of every game "day" or episode |
| **Solution #2** | **Constantly reinforce** player engagement through plot/character-based choices |
| **Result** | Diverse narrative reinforcement for all players leads to informed, engaged, loyal player base |

### Three Important Takeaways（#56）
1. On a long-enough timeline, **everyone will stop playing your game**.
2. It's in your power to give them enough **narrative coherence** to keep them invested.
3. You must **stay out of the player's way** as much as you can while giving them the context they need.

### Concluding Best Practices（#57）
- We can't rely on the player **remembering everything**
- Know **when and where** to deliver story/lore — moment-to-moment versus safe space
- Know the **genre's expectations** — but some trappings are universal
- **Simpler is better** — Use proper names and locations in UI
- **Test as much as possible** to see why/when players get lost or disengaged

## キーコンセプト整理

### Narrative Coherence（ナラティブ・コヒーレンス）
- ゲームデザイン・メカニクスと巧みに連携させながら、プレイヤーをナラティブに引き込み続ける技術
- すべてのゲームにナラティブがある（Space Invaders でさえ）

### 6つのケーススタディ — 共通パターン: Problem → Solution → Result

| # | ゲーム | ジャンル | 核心的解決策 |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| 1 | **Fable 2** | アクションRPG | Sparkle path + ambient narrative。フレーバーテキストはメニューへ |
| 2 | **Guild Wars 2** | MMO | 戦闘中は bare-bones、safe zone で lore journal |
| 3 | **Destiny** | FPS-RPG | GW2 の arena/safe zone 戦略をジャンル横断で応用 |
| 4 | **Forza Motorsport 7** | レーシング | Light touch。冒頭にナラティブ、残りはプレイヤーの体験 |
| 5 | **Horizon Forbidden West** | オープンワールドACT | Brief UI + NPC/audio log を非戦闘エリアに配置 |
| 6 | **Lily's Garden** | モバイルパズル | エピソード開始/終了時にリマインド + プロット選択で reinforcement |

### 横断的教訓
1. **Moment-to-moment vs Safe space**: 戦闘中は最小限の情報、安全な場所で深い情報を提供
2. **ジャンル横断の応用**: 成功パターンは別ジャンルでも使える（GW2 → Destiny）
3. **プレイヤーの邪魔をするな**: Light touch で期待と体験を統合
4. **プレイヤーの記憶に頼るな**: 常にリマインド・リインフォースメントを設計

### 前3人のスピーカーとの接続
- **Schreiber の認知負荷理論** → Ryan の「moment-to-moment は bare-bones」= Extraneous Load の最小化
- **Ash の Tutorial Matrix** → Ryan の「safe zone に情報を配置」= SHOW（Diegetic/Just-in-time）
- **Fan の「Ask less, Give more」** → Ryan の「stay out of the player's way while giving context」
- **全員共通**: プレイヤーの認知を尊重し、適切なタイミングで適切な量の情報を提供する
